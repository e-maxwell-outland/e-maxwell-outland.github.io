I find myself in a cycle of working, adulting, and filling my spare time with just watching something. Sure, I do yoga and BJJ to exercise, albeit less consistently than I'd like. I also intermittently play games and read books whenever my fascination drifts that way. But most days? "Doing something" beyond just existing after all the things that are pressing for my attention just feels too tiring. So I chase the time away mindlessly watching and listening to what someone else has to say. My only solace is that at least I'm not scrolling.

Yet even still, I feel like time is slipping through my hands that way; I finally look up and the hours passed me by with barely a whisper. To feel time moving a little more slowly, I'm trying to find little ways to stay engaged with my life that don't take much energy. I thought I would write "postcard fiction": exactly 100 words about whatever little story I want. For the first one, I thought I would give you a little glimpse into my cats' relationship with our plants...

---

Green, supple leaves glint in the dim rainy light. Little yellow eyes are intently fixed on the verdant branches. The ball of grey fur stretches and retracts, paws like reaching vines. A thin tail swishes like a tree in the wind. In an explosion, four tiny mauve feet land on the table by the window, the delicious forest of green within a stride. A velvet black nose twitches and trembles, taking in the earthy scent. From above, great hands reach down, grasping and carrying the small creature away. Green, supple leaves glint in the dim rainy light, quiet and undisturbed.
