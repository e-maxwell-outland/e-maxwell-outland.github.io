The question at the center of my work right now is deceptively simple: what does it mean for an autonomous system to *explain* itself?

When a path planner routes around an obstacle, the decision is legible to the engineer who built the system. It's usually invisible to the person depending on it. A saliency heatmap makes the geometry visible, but "the robot went left because this region had high density" isn't the same as understanding why that path was the right choice — or whether to trust it.

The gap between *interpretable* and *understandable* is where a lot of human-robot trust actually breaks down. I'm spending most of my time thinking about that gap.
