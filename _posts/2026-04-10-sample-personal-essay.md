I started Brazilian Jiu Jitsu because I was told it was a good idea to know how to defend yourself. I stayed because there is almost nothing in my life that provides the same immediate, undeniable feedback as being submitted by someone half my size.

In research, you can tell yourself a half-formed idea is almost there. You can sit with uncertainty for months. The model might be wrong but the data haven't caught up yet. There's always another interpretation.

On the mat, you either escaped or you didn't.

I'm still mostly in the "didn't" category. But I'm learning that being bad at something — truly, visibly, physically bad — is one of the more honest experiences available. You can't theorize your way out of a triangle choke.

I think there's something in that worth holding onto, especially in a field that rewards having a sophisticated take on everything.
